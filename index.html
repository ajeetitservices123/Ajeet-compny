English grammar ka yeh topic ek thrilling game ki tarah hai! Iska basic concept bahut easy hai: Aapko bas Subject aur Verb ko ek doosre ke sath match karna hai. Learning ke is safar mein enjoy the process!

1. The Golden Matching Rule

Sentence mein balance banaye rakhne ke liye ek simple niyam follow karein. Ise seekhna sach mein bada enjoyment deta hai:

  • Agar Subject Singular (Ek vachan) hai, toh uski Verb bhi Singular hogi (verb ke end mein -s ya -es lagate hain).
  • Agar Subject Plural (Bahu vachan) hai, toh uski Verb bhi Plural hogi (isme -s nahi lagta, base form use hoti hai).
Examples dekhiye:

Singular: The student reads a book. (Student kitaab padhta hai.)
Plural: The students read a book. (Students kitaab padhte hain.)

2. The "And" Magic Team

Jab do alag-alag subjects word "and" se judte hain, toh wo milkar ek plural team ban jate hain. Is trick ko use karna bahut easy hai.

Example: Rahul and Priya are going to the market.
(Dono milkar plural ban gaye, isliye 'is' ki jagah 'are' lagaya.)

3. "Each aur Every" Ka Rule

Dhyan rahe, words jaise Each, Every, Everyone, Somebody hamesha ek unit (Singular) maane jate hain. Inke sath hamesha singular verb hi lagani chahiye.

Example: Everyone is happy today.
(Yahan 'Everyone' singular hai, isliye 'is' ka use hua hai.)

In basic rules ko dhyan mein rakh kar aap koi bhi sentence bina galti ke bana sakte hain. Grammar ke is game ko jeetna ab aapke baayein hath ka khel hai!
